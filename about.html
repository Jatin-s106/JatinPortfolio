Third-year student, first-year builder mindset.

A little about how I got here and what I actually spend my time doing.

I'm a third-year BSCIT (Bachelor of Science in Information Technology) student, currently working through the core of my degree while spending most of my free time building things outside of class.

My coursework has given me a base in programming fundamentals, databases, and software concepts — but the part I enjoy most is taking that theory and turning it into something that actually runs. That's what led to LanceFree, a freelancing platform I designed and built myself, including its login and account system.

I like picking a real, slightly messy problem and building a working version of the solution — even if the first version is rough. I'd rather ship something imperfect and improve it than keep it purely theoretical.

Right now I'm focused on strengthening my full-stack fundamentals — cleaner backend logic, better database design, and more comfort across the languages and tools I already use day to day.
